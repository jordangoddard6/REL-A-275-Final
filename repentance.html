Repentance

The word "repentance" is used twice as much in the Book of Mormon than in any other book of canonical scripture. This simple fact alone can teach us many things about repentance.

The Fulness of the Gospel

We are taught that the Book of Mormon contains the fulness of the gospel of Jesus Christ. This idea, combined with the fact that the Book of Mormon teaches repentance more than any book of scripture shows to us the importance of repentance to the gospel of Jesus Christ. In one of the clearest teachers of Christ's gospel, he chose to put a hard emphasis on repentance. This should teach us how important it is to Him.

Teachings for Our Day

We know that the Book of Mormon was written for our day. Much of the Book of Mormon follows the Nephite and Lamanite societies leading up to the coming of Christ. We, likewise, are living in a society that is approaching the coming of Christ. Thus, what is taught to those people is likewise important to us.

Repentance is taught heavily to these people. It is a protection that allows us to be safe from Satan's influence and be prepared for the Savior's coming. Clearly God is calling us to repent as well, or we may risk facing the same consequences that many unrepentant souls faced in the Book of Mormon.

Applications

  • Repent daily
  • Pray to know how we can be better
  • Read the Book of Mormon looking for how repentance can bless our lives
